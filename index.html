Computational Biology at the Intersection of Medicine

Dr. Piyush Kumar Yadav is an Assistant Professor of Bioinformatics at Lovely Professional University, Punjab. His research bridges computational chemistry and molecular biology to accelerate drug discovery for underserved diseases.

His doctoral and post-doctoral work focused on β-thalassemia major — a severe blood disorder — employing 3D-QSAR, pharmacophore modelling, molecular docking and MD simulations to identify novel iron chelation and cardiac arrhythmia targets. He has extended this computational framework to cancer, HIV, and oral pathogens.

With publications in journals carrying impact factors up to 7.0 and an award for Best Scientist at a national conference, Dr. Yadav brings rigorous computational approaches to clinically impactful problems.
